Pumpkin Churros

Dessert

Pumpkin season is here, and I had to turn one of my favorite fall flavors into something fun and delicious! These pumpkin-shaped churros are crispy, warm, and loaded with pumpkin spice goodness—basically fall in every bite. Trust me, you’ll want to make a batch because they’ll disappear in seconds!


Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Total Time: 1 hour

Yields: 10 to 12 churros

Pumpkin Churros

If fall could be wrapped up in a dessert, it would definitely be these pumpkin-shaped churros. They’re crisp on the outside, tender on the inside, and dusted in a cinnamon sugar mix with a hint of pumpkin spice to really drive home that cozy fall vibe. Whether you’re serving these at a gathering or just enjoying them with a hot cup of coffee, these churros are going to be a hit

Cinnamon Sugar Mixture

Before you start making the churro dough and frying, you’ll want to make your cinnamon sugar mixture. It’s a simple blend of sugar, cinnamon, and pumpkin pie spice. This mix is going to give the churros that sweet, spiced coating reminiscent of pumpkin pie. Make sure to set it aside so it’s ready for when your churros come out of the fryer. You can adjust the level of spice depending on how strong you want that pumpkin flavor to be—go lighter for a more subtle touch, or add an extra dash if you want it to really pop!

Churro Batter

The churro batter comes together on the stovetop and is surprisingly easy. Start by heating water, sugar, vanilla, pumpkin puree, butter, and a pinch of salt until it all comes together in a smooth mixture. Once it’s boiling, you’ll stir in the flour, making sure it’s fully combined. A rubber spatula works best here—you want a smooth, lump-free dough. After cooking the dough for a couple of minutes to dry it out slightly, let it cool for a few minutes. This step is important because adding eggs to a hot dough will scramble them, and we don’t want that! Once the dough has cooled a bit, you’ll mix in the eggs one at a time. The dough should transform into something soft, elastic, and just a bit sticky. If it feels too wet, you can let it sit for a few minutes to firm up. A stand mixer makes this process quicker, but you can definitely do it by hand with a rubber spatula if you’re up for it!

TIP: For even more flavor, brown the butter first and then add the water, sugar, vanilla, pumpkin puree, and a pinch of salt. This adds a delicious nutty flavor!

Piping the Pumpkins

Now for the fun part—shaping the churros! You’ll need a piping bag fitted with a star tip to create that iconic churro texture. Pipe out your pumpkins onto small squares of parchment paper. Make sure the dough is thick enough to hold its shape but not too thick to pipe smoothly. If the “stems” don’t look quite right, you can snip them off with scissors before frying. It’s helpful to have all your churros piped out before you start frying to keep things moving quickly. You can find the piping tip at a store like Michaels or purchase it online from Amazon here. Also, if piping the pumpkin shape turns out too difficult you can just pipe it into a typical churro shape right into the hot oil!

Frying Tips

Getting the oil temperature right is crucial for perfect churros. You want it hot enough to give the churros that golden brown crunch, but not too hot! If the oil is too hot, the churros will brown quickly on the outside but stay raw on the inside. Aim for an even temperature and fry them in small batches—this helps ensure each churro cooks through evenly. Be sure to flip them halfway through so they get golden brown on both sides. Once they’re fried to perfection, transfer the churros to a paper towel to drain excess oil. You’ll want to fry them for about 4 to 5 minutes on each side to ensure they’re fully cooked.

PRO TIP: Keep an eye on your oil temperature between batches. If it cools down too much, the churros will absorb more oil, and no one wants greasy churros!

Pumpkin season is here, and I had to turn one of my favorite fall flavors into something fun and delicious! These pumpkin-shaped churros are crispy, warm, and loaded with pumpkin spice goodness—basically fall in every bite. Trust me, you’ll want to make a batch because they’ll disappear in seconds!


Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 30 minutes

Total Time: 1 hour

Yields: 10 to 12 churros

Ingredients

CINNAMON SUGAR MIXTURE
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice
  • CHURRO BATTER
  • 3/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • /2 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • vegetable oil, for frying

OPTIONAL DIPPING SAUCES

  • caramel sauce
  • chocolate sauce
  • sweetened condensed milk

Instructions

      In a small bowl, whisk together sugar and cinnamon to make the cinnamon-sugar mixture. Set aside.
      In a pot, add water, sugar, vanilla extract, pumpkin puree, unsalted butter, and salt.
    Set over medium-high heat and whisk together.
      Once it comes to a boil, drop the heat to low and add the flour. Use a rubber spatula to mix together until there are no more clumps of flour left, about 3 minutes.
        Take off the heat and allow to cool for 6-8 minutes.
          Add one egg and mix using a rubber spatula or in a stand mixer until fully incorporated. Add the second egg and mix again until fully incorporated. The dough should be firm, elastic and just a bit sticky.
            Transfer into a piping bag fitted with a tip.
              Cut parchment paper into small squares. Spray with oil and pipe pumpkins out onto each square. You may need to use scissors to cut the stem off at the end.
